Recognize the Signs


Among those who have been trafficked, you can notice a few consistencies. People who have been trafficked are usually very anxious and submissive. They usually try to avoid any confrontation, but particularly fear the authorities. If he/she is addressed in a situation, the victim will try his/her best to try and redirect the conversation from himself/herself. Occasionally, ‘owners’ of someone being trafficked label victims with tattoo’s of things like names or bar codes. Lastly, of those who are in the prostitution ring will most commonly be dressed in a sexualized manner, without age being taken into account. Please note, that if you notice any of these signs, do not immediately assume one is being trafficked. Call the proper authorities if you have seen legitimate reason to be suspicious of another.

Awareness is Key


Human trafficking is officially defined by the Oxford Pocket English Dictionary as “the action or practice of illegally transporting people from one country or area to another, typically for the purposes of forced labour or commercial sexual exploitation.”, but modern day slavery describes it better. People have their rights forcefully taken from them and practically become objects for people to buy and use. A common misconception about human trafficking is that only children are taken. Anyone, despite their age, race, gender, sexuality, etc., can be taken away. Awareness is necessary so people can not only be prepared to protect themselves but take notice of someone who might be suffering.



The Facts

- People brought into the Human Trafficking circle have their human rights abused with forced labor, prostitution, removal of organs, and many other things that are offered among the black market


- In a few cases, human trafficking provides spouses with forced marriage, mostly providing people with very large age differences


- Human trafficking occurs IN and OUT of criminal organization groups


- It is estimated that Human Trafficking produces around $150 billion in profit as of 2014


- In 2012, 21 million people had their rights taken away from them as they were entered into the Human Trafficking business, 68% entered into forced labor, 22% in forced prostitution, and 10% forcefully placed in military positions in groups like rebel armies and militaries


- January is designated as National Slavery and Human Trafficking Prevention Month


- Atlanta, Georgia is the largest hub in America for human trafficking due to its airports


- Sexually exploited individuals also commonly find themselves in the drug trafficking ring the longer they are in the prostitution ring


- People of all ages abducted regardless of being local or abroad
